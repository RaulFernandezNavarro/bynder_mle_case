# Search by Image – Bynder Support

**Source URL:** https://support.bynder.com/hc/en-us/articles/13812016590354

Before you purchase or upload a new image to your DAM, you can search your Asset Bank to see if a similar or exact one already exists via the Search by Image functionality. In the [search bar](https://support.bynder.com/hc/en-us/articles/360013931359-Use-the-Search-Bar-to-Find-Assets)
, you can upload an image stored on your computer or add an external image URL to search for a similar asset in your DAM.

This feature/solution requires your Customer Success Contact to enable, but then individual permissions can be done by the Bynder Admin.

### Note

Any permission restrictions set on an asset will be honored and will affect if an asset is retrievable in the search.

Search by an Uploaded Image[](https://support.bynder.com/hc/en-us/articles/13812016590354#h_01HAN7CA86VHX5KSTWSDG285Y2)

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1.  Navigate to the Asset Bank.
2.  Click ![](https://support.bynder.com/hc/article_attachments/15129094048530) in the top right corner. ![Screenshot 2023-11-17 at 2.44.34 PM.png](https://support.bynder.com/hc/article_attachments/15159082172562)
3.  Click **Upload** to select an image stored on your computer (JPG, JPEG, PNG, WEBP). ![d6869fa6-77fc-4c0f-b674-ea8098f359b8](https://support.bynder.com/hc/article_attachments/15129109159954)
4.   The most similar assets will be displayed.
5.  To remove the similarity search, click **Clear** **Filters** or the **x** next to **Filtering on Visual similarity.**

### Note

Images used in your search will not be uploaded to your Bynder DAM.

### How to Copy and Paste Image Links

If you are using Chrome, Firefox, or Safari, then you have two ways to copy the URL of the image.![af0cec50-ac51-491b-8c62-9d60fe398c46](https://support.bynder.com/hc/article_attachments/15159082179090)

### Note

To make sure you properly copied the URL, check the URL extension. The extension should have either a . JPG, .PNG, WEBP, or .JPEG at the end of the URL.

### Option 1

1.  Right-click the image.
2.  In the dropdown menu, click **Copy Image Address. ![](https://support.bynder.com/hc/article_attachments/15159082180114)** 

### Option 2

1.  Right-click the image.
2.  In the dropdown menu click **Open** Im**a**ge in New Tab, which will open the image in a separate tab.
3.  Copy the URL that appears in your browser.![](https://support.bynder.com/hc/article_attachments/15159069914898)

### Internet Explorer

1.  If you are using Internet Explorer, then you will need to follow these steps to copy the link of the image:
2.  Right-click the image.
3.  Select **Properties**.
4.  Navigate to the Address (URL).
5.  Highlight and copy the entire URL.

**Will the results include images from PDFs or document files?**

No, the search will focus exclusively on image assets available in your Asset Bank. It does not extract or search for images within PDFs or document files.

**Why does this feature not work from my homepage?**

This feature is only usable in the search bar of the asset bank.
